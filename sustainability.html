Here's What We Can Do To Save the Earth

Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

Help reduce carbon emissions by taking walks, going for cycles and taking public transport, instead of using private or personal vehicles, every gallon of gasoline you save avoids 22 pounds of CO2 emissions

Donate to Green Organisations

By donating to green organisations, you provide them with financial support which in turns allows them to fund their environmental conservation efforts.

Take Part in Environmental Activities

Participating in environmental activities often involves engaging in conservation efforts such as planting trees, restoring habitats of animals, or doing cleanups which helps contributes to the environment.

Recycle waste

Recycling waste such as paper reduces the need for deforestation in order to produce more paper which not only save our trees but also prevent greenhouse gases from being released from the burning of forests.

Reduce Waste

Reducing the waste you dipose of helps to conserve the space in our landfills, reducing the need to build even more landfill to accomodate to the built-up waste, that would have caused air and water pollution, while taking up our valuable space.

Reuse Household Items

Reusing your household items helps to limits the need to buy new materials, saving you money and reducing waste by delaying disposal. This helps to conserve our raw materials, prevents overuse of resources and preserves them.

Personal Goals

I pledge to go sustainable by:

  • Bringing my own tote bags instead of using single-use plastic bags when going shopping.
  • Taking public transport or cycling instead of hiring a cab to reduce my carbon footprint.
  • Saving energy by turning off electrical appliance when not in use.
  • Purchasing goods and services from companies that use sustainable and environmentally friendly products.
  • Educate my family and friends on how and why they should lead a sustainable lifestyle.
  • Reducing food watse by choosing portion sizes wisely and taking only what I can eat.

What Singapore has done

Launched by Ministry of Environment in 2002, it aimed to generate 60% of Singapore’s energy needs using natural gas by 2012. Natural gas is a cleaner form of energy in comparison to coal because it does not produce smoke, resulting in lower carbon emission. It was successful as 79% of Singapore energy was generated from natural gas as early as 2010 which exceeded the Ministry of Environments target ahead of schedule.

Started by the Garden City Fund and Singapore Environment Council in 1997 as Tree planting day, it aims to maintain Singapore’s status as a Garden City by planting trees, residents are encouraged to donate money or volunteer in tree planting events held monthly throughout Singapore. More trees planted can help to take in carbon dioxide which is a greenhouse gas that traps heat,the programme has contributed to an estimated 60,000 trees planted throughout Singapore yearly by the National Park Boards.

Launched by Building Construction Authority in 2005, the scheme evaluates and certifies building according to how energy-effecient and environmentally friendly they are. It encourages more new ‘green buildings to be constructed, that uses less energy and provide the same service, such as LED lighting or those running partly on solar energy. Existing ‘green’ building such as National Library Building, are 30% more energy efficient than other buildings in Singapore It uses vertical greenery which reduces solar heat gain, resuling in less air conditioning to keep the building cool, cutting down carbon emissions.
