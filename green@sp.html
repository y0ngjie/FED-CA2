Green @ SP

Sustainability in SP

Find out more on how SP goes Green

SP's Pledge

"We pledge to green our campus , reduce our environmental impact and nurture stewards of sustainability"


Progress

Green Mark Platinum Buildings

SP aims to certify all 22 buildings Green Mark Platinum by 2025, with 4 buidlings already certified.

230 Species of Flora

SP's campus is home to an astonishing 230 different variety of plants, making it not only green but beautiful as well.

110+ Water Dispensers

With over 110+ water dispenese located throughout the campus, students are encouraged to bring their own water bottles instead of singe-use platic bottles.

500+ Smart Sensors

Despite the size of its large campus, SP is able to monitor human-traficcing and air quality in order to cater to the students and staffs on campus.

4 Smart Offices

The work environment is improved for the students and staffs through the use of temperature sensors and better air quality.

13 Diplomas featuring Common Core Curriculum (CCC)

In order to learn more about the Green Economy, students are exposed to cross-disciplinary learning opportunities and global issues, which are framed by the SG Green Plan and the Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nations.

Initiatives

SP X SG Clean Day

SP invited its students to go down and join SG Clean Day in order to make Singapore a cleaner and better home for everyone. This year they teamed up to to set a new Singapore Book of Records entry for the most number of people in an island-wide clean-up which was achieved with almost 4,500 volunteers turning up.

SP Environment Club

The Singapore Poly Environmental Club is a CCA group concerned about the natural environment. The club is commited to promoting awarness on the environment and leading sustainable lifestyles not only on campus, but outside as well. They organise various green activities and projects in order to make SP a greener place.

Collaborations & Patnership

SP collabes with Sembcorp and CHINT to work together accelerate the progress of sustainable energy development in Asia.

Leading energy companies Sembcorp Industries (Sembcorp) and CHINT are collaborating to create an Environmental Sustainability Platform, in order to help companies and consumers to achieve their sustainability goals.

A sustainability collaboration has been formed, ink (MoU) with A*Star, JTC, and TUV SUD.

These agencies are collaborating to further develop the Green Compass environmental sustainability assessment and roadmapping tool and bring it to more companies in Singapore. The tool aims to help businesses become more resource effecient, adaptable to sustainability transformation and helps them understand their environmental progress.

Singapore Polytechnic and its partners will take lead in providing Industry 4.0 solutions for SMEs and help them develop smart and sustainable businesses.

SP will sign 4 MoUs to empower and help SME's convert their buisness digitally, bolster operations effeciency, as well as improve in sustainable and smart environments.
